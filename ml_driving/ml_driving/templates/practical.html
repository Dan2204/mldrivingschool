Practical Driving Test

On Average, according to the DVSA, most people will have about 47 hours of professional driving lessons and 20 hours of private practice before they pass their practical diving test.

Everyone learns at a different pace and this is just an average, but it goes to show that it's clearly important to get plenty of practice prior to taking your practical driving test.

Booking Your Practical Test

Once you have completed your Theory Test and you're ready to take your Practical Test, you can visit GOV.UK to book your test online.

Things you'll need when booking a practical test:

  • A valid UK provisional driving license number.
  • A credit or Debit Card to pay for the test.
  • Your theory test pass date and certificate number.
  • Your driving instructors personal reference number to confirm they are available.

* When booking, please keep in mind that the date you would like to book can vary depending on how busy the centre is. Some centres could be fully booked up to 3 months in advance.

The cost of the test is £62 on weekdays and £75 on evenings and weekends.

The Day Of Your Test:

Make sure you are fully prepared and confident in everything you have learned. Your instructor will pick you up around an hour prior to your test. This will give time to warm up and ask any questions you may have thought of.

* Remember to bring along your provisional license and theory test pass certificate.

Your test will last about 40 minutes and will include:

  • An eyesight check.
  • A 'show me, tell me' test.
  • 1 Driving manoeuvre: forward bay parking, pull up on the right, reverse parking or reverse bay parking.
  • Emergency stop.
  • 20 Minutes of independent driving.

At the end of the test, your examiner will tell you if you have passed or failed.

If you have passed, you will receive a Test Pass Certificate which will entitle you to drive imediately.
Your driving instructor will usually drive you back home afterwards so you can relax and enjoy your success.

However, if you have failed, your examiner will produce a test report, and explain the errors you made, so that you know what you still need to work on to pass when you take the test again.

Gaining the freedom to drive yourself to the places you want to go, whenever you want to is exciting. We aim to help get you there as quickly (and safely) as possible.

We will tell you what your strengths and weaknesses are and what the examiner will be looking out for.

Manoeuvres are important, but observing and understanding the
situations you find yourself in are what really matters.
