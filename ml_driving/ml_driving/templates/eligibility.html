Provisional Licence

Before you can start learning to drive on the roads, you must hold a UK provisional driving licence.

You can apply for this in person at your local post office, or online.

Once you have received your provisional licence, you will be legally eligible to start driving from your 17th birthday onwards.

On your first lesson, you will need to bring your provisional license along with a DVSA code which proves your entitlement to drive.

You will also need to be able to read a new-style number plate from 20 metres, or an old-style number plate from 20.5 metres. (Please check the legal eyesight requirements here)

Requirements for applying for your provisional licence:

  • You can apply 3 months before you turn 17, but you need to wait until your birthday before you can drive on the road
  • You'll need a valid proof of identification
  • You must live in the UK
  • You will need to provide all the addresses you have lived for the last 3 years
  • You can't be prevented from driving for any reason
  • The cost will be £34 if paid online, or £43 if applying by post

Please wear sensible shoes and clothing for your lessons.
This allows you to drive safely without distractions.
