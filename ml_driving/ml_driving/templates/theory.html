Theory & Hazard Perception Tests

The DVSA theory test consists of 2 sections:

  • Multiple Choice Test
  • Hazard Perception Test

Both are to be completed together at your nearest test center.
You need to pass both to be successful.

We recommend that you study for the Theory and Hazard Perception Tests whilst taking practical lessons, which will reinforce your studies and put to action what you have leaned.

Study and Booking

Theory Test:

The Theory Test is a Multiple Choice formatted exam and is completed on a computer. It is designed to increase knowledge of road safety and rules of the road. The pass mark is 43 of 50 questions.

Your instructor will help you with your theory during your lessons to help ensure you have the knowledge to pass.

You can purchase an array of books, DVDs, apps to help you prepare. We recommend taking your Theory Test as soon as possible so you can concentrate on the practical lessons.

Also ensure you revise The Highway Code, and familiarise yourself with road safety and rules of the road.

Hazard Perception Test:

The Hazard Perception Test is designed to identify and predict potential dangers and hazards on the road, from a drivers perspective. This test will also be completed on a computer, clicking the mouse to identify hazards as they appear on the screen.

There are 15 hazards to identify, with a maximum of score of 5 points each (if you manage to click the mouse as the hazard starts to develop). The pass mark is 44 out of 75 points.

You can practice with 3 clips for free here.

  • If you fail either the Hazard Perception or Theory Tests, you will be required to take them both again. You will also have to wait for 3 days before taking another test.
  • Once you have passed, your certificate will be valid for 2 years from the date you passed.
    You will need to take your Practical Test within these 2 years, otherwise you will be required to take both tests again.

Booking a Test:

Booking a test is quick and easy, visit the booking page on GOV.UK, the process should take about 5 minutes.

Tests cost Â£23 and you will need to hold a UK provisional Licence to book.

You will also need to make sure you have the following to hand when booking:

  • Your provisional driving licence number
  • A credit or debit card to pay for the test
  • Details of any special needs as you will have to mention what they are during the booking process. DVSA can arrange for more time on the multiple choice questions or any other reasonable arrangements (you will be required to provide proof)

On the day of the test, make sure you bring along your provisional photocard licence, you won't be allowed to sit the test without it and will lose your fee.

Good luck if you're sitting your theory test soon!
